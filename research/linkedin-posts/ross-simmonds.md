## Post 2 — Create Once, Distribute Forever

**Date:** 2024  
**Theme:** Content distribution framework

> Most B2B SaaS companies create content and then do nothing with it.
> 
> They publish a blog post. Maybe share it once on LinkedIn.
> Then move on.
> 
> The best content teams I know operate differently.
> They create once and distribute forever.
> 
> One piece of content becomes:
> → A LinkedIn post series
> → A Reddit thread with genuine insight
> → A YouTube short or full video
> → A Twitter/X thread
> → A newsletter segment
> → A podcast talking point
> 
> Same idea. 7 different surfaces. 7 different audiences.
> And each placement increases the chances an LLM cites you.

**Key insight:** Distribution multiplier — one content asset deployed across 7 surfaces increases both human reach and AI training data exposure.

## Post 3 — Why Your Content Isn't Getting Cited by AI

**Date:** 2025  
**Theme:** GEO practical advice

> Quick test: go ask ChatGPT about your category.
> Does your brand appear?
> 
> If not, here's why:
> 
> 1. Your content only lives on your domain
> 2. LLMs weight community platforms heavily (Reddit, Quora, LinkedIn)
> 3. You haven't built enough topical authority signals off-site
> 
> Fix:
> → Engage on Reddit with genuine expertise (not promotion)
> → Write LinkedIn articles, not just posts
> → Get cited on podcasts and YouTube (transcripts matter)
> 
> The brands I see winning in AI search are the ones who understood distribution first.

**Key insight:** Brands invisible in AI-generated answers typically have domain-only content presence. Off-site distribution on community platforms drives AI citations.
