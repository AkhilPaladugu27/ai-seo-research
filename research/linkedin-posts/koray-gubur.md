## Post 2 — How to Build a Semantic Content Network

**Date:** 2024
**Theme:** Practical topical authority implementation

> Most SEOs build pages. I build semantic networks.
>
> The difference:
>
> A page answers one query.
> A semantic network answers a topic domain.
>
> How to build one:
> 1. Map every entity in your topic (people, tools, concepts, processes)
> 2. Create content for each entity — not keyword-stuffed, entity-defined
> 3. Connect related entities through internal links with semantic anchor text
> 4. Fill gaps — if AI asks about your topic, can it find the answer on your site?
>
> When your site becomes the most complete semantic map of a topic,
> AI systems have no choice but to cite you.

**Key insight:** Semantic network approach — map entities, define them, connect them, fill gaps. Completeness drives AI citations.

## Post 3 — AI Agents for SEO Gaps

**Date:** 2025
**Theme:** Using AI to find missing content in semantic networks

> I built an AI agent that crawls my clients' sites and identifies semantic gaps.
>
> It asks: "What entities exist in this topic domain that this site doesn't cover?"
>
> The output is a content brief list — not based on keyword volume,
> but based on what's missing from the semantic map.
>
> This is how AI-powered SEO content production should work:
> → AI identifies gaps in your topical coverage
> → Humans decide which gaps matter
> → AI assists in filling them with entity-rich content
> → Humans review for accuracy and E-E-A-T signals
>
> Volume is not the goal. Completeness is.

**Key insight:** AI agents identify semantic gaps → humans prioritize → AI fills → humans verify. Completeness over volume.
