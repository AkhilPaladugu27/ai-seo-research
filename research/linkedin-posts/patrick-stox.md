## Post 1 — Making Your Site LLM-Ready

**Date:** 2024
**Theme:** Technical foundations for AI search visibility

> "LLM-ready" is the new "mobile-friendly."
>
> Here's what it actually means technically:
>
> → Structured data (Schema.org) helps AI parse entity relationships
> → Clean HTML without JavaScript rendering issues = easier for AI to process
> → Clear heading hierarchy (H1 → H2 → H3) defines content chunks
> → Semantic HTML elements (article, section, nav) signal content structure
> → Fast load times matter — AI crawlers have budgets too
>
> Most sites fail on structured data and heading hierarchy.
> Fix those two first. Everything else is incremental.

**Key insight:** LLM-ready = structured data + clean HTML + clear heading hierarchy + semantic HTML + fast load. Structured data and headings are highest priority fixes.

## Post 2 — What Ahrefs Data Shows About AI Content

**Date:** 2024
**Theme:** Aggregate data insights on AI-generated content performance

> We see data from millions of sites at Ahrefs.
>
> What the data shows about AI content in 2024:
>
> → Sites that publish AI content without editorial review
>   show higher bounce rates and lower dwell time
> → Pages with clear author attribution outperform anonymous AI content
>   even when the content quality is similar
> → Internal linking from AI-generated pages is often weaker —
>   AI doesn't naturally reference other site content
> → AI content that includes original data outperforms
>   AI content that summarizes existing information
>
> The pattern: AI content succeeds when it looks like human content.
> Not in style — in signals. Author, links, data, structure.

**Key insight:** Ahrefs aggregate data — AI content underperforms when: no editorial review, no author attribution, weak internal linking, no original data. Fix all four.

## Post 3 — Structured Data for AI Search

**Date:** 2025
**Theme:** Which Schema types matter most for AI visibility

> Not all structured data is equal for AI search.
>
> Priority Schema types for B2B SaaS AI SEO:
>
> 1. Article / BlogPosting — author, date, publisher signals
> 2. FAQPage — directly maps to conversational AI queries
> 3. HowTo — step-by-step content AI loves to cite
> 4. Organization — entity definition for your brand
> 5. Person — author entity definition (crucial for E-E-A-T)
> 6. Product — for SaaS feature pages
> 7. BreadcrumbList — helps AI understand site structure
>
> Most B2B SaaS sites have zero of these implemented correctly.
> Start with Article + FAQPage. Those two cover 80% of the impact.

**Key insight:** 7 Schema types for B2B SaaS AI SEO. Start with Article + FAQPage — covers 80% of impact. Author and brand entity definition critical for E-E-A-T signals.
