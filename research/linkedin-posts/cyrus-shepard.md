## Post 1 — Title Tag Experiment (500K Pages)

**Date:** 2024
**Theme:** What actually changes CTR — data from massive experiment

> I ran a title tag experiment across 500,000 pages.
>
> Here's what actually moves CTR:
>
> → Adding the current year: +5-8% CTR lift
> → Adding a number ("7 ways"): +4-6% lift
> → Front-loading the keyword: +3-5% lift
> → Adding brackets [Guide] or (2024): +3-4% lift
> → Making it a question: mixed results, topic-dependent
>
> What DOESN'T help as much as people think:
> → Power words ("Ultimate", "Complete", "Best")
> → Emoji in titles — minimal lift, sometimes negative
>
> Test everything. Don't trust SEO opinions including mine.
> The data from YOUR site is the only truth that matters.

**Key insight:** Data from 500K page experiment — year, numbers, front-loaded keywords drive CTR. Power words overrated. Always test on your own site.

## Post 2 — Internal Linking Experiment Results

**Date:** 2024
**Theme:** How internal links affect rankings — controlled test

> We ran a controlled internal linking experiment.
>
> Added 3-5 internal links to 200 pages that had zero internal links pointing to them.
> Compared to a control group of similar pages with no changes.
>
> Results after 60 days:
> → Test group: average +23% organic traffic
> → Control group: flat
>
> Internal links are underrated. Most sites have orphaned pages
> that could rank with zero new content — just better internal linking.
>
> Before producing new AI content:
> Check if your existing content is properly linked.
> A well-linked old page often beats a new page.

**Key insight:** Internal linking experiment — adding 3-5 links to orphaned pages drove +23% organic traffic. Fix internal links before producing new content.

## Post 3 — How to Run SEO Experiments

**Date:** 2025
**Theme:** Methodology for testing SEO changes

> Most SEO "experiments" aren't experiments. They're anecdotes.
>
> A real SEO experiment requires:
>
> 1. CONTROL GROUP — pages you don't change
> 2. TEST GROUP — similar pages where you make one change
> 3. SINGLE VARIABLE — change only one thing at a time
> 4. SUFFICIENT SAMPLE — minimum 100 pages per group
> 5. SUFFICIENT TIME — minimum 30 days, ideally 60+
> 6. STATISTICAL SIGNIFICANCE — don't celebrate noise
>
> Without these, you don't have data. You have a story.
>
> The SEO industry runs on stories.
> Run experiments instead.

**Key insight:** 6-step SEO experiment framework — control group, test group, single variable, 100+ pages, 60 days, statistical significance. Most "experiments" skip all six.
