## Post 1 — AI Content Production Workflow

**Date:** 2024
**Theme:** How to produce AI content that survives Google updates

> Everyone asks me: "Can I use AI to produce SEO content?"
>
> Yes. But not the way most people do it.
>
> The workflow that works:
>
> 1. RESEARCH first — AI cannot replace primary research
> 2. OUTLINE with human expertise — structure based on actual knowledge
> 3. DRAFT with AI assistance — use AI to speed up writing, not replace thinking
> 4. EDIT for accuracy — fact-check every claim AI makes
> 5. ADD experience signals — first-hand examples, data, opinions
> 6. REVIEW for E-E-A-T — would a subject matter expert be proud of this?
>
> The sites that got hit by HCU skipped steps 1, 2, 4, 5, and 6.
> They only did step 3.

**Key insight:** 6-step AI content workflow — research, outline, draft, edit, add experience, E-E-A-T review. Most penalized sites only did the draft step.

## Post 2 — Content Audit Before AI Production

**Date:** 2024
**Theme:** Why audit comes before scale

> Before you produce 100 AI-assisted articles, do this:
>
> Audit what you already have.
>
> Most B2B SaaS sites have:
> → 40% of pages getting zero traffic
> → 30% of pages cannibalizing each other
> → 20% of pages that could rank with minor updates
> → 10% of pages actually driving results
>
> Adding 100 more articles to a broken foundation = 100 more problems.
>
> Fix the 20% first. Update them with AI assistance.
> Then use AI to fill genuine gaps — topics you don't cover at all.
>
> Scale without audit = scaling chaos.

**Key insight:** Audit before scaling. Fix existing content first (highest ROI), then use AI to fill genuine topical gaps — not add to an already bloated site.

## Post 3 — International AI SEO Framework

**Date:** 2025
**Theme:** AI SEO across languages and markets

> AI search behaves differently across languages and markets.
>
> What works in English SEO doesn't always translate.
>
> Key differences to account for:
> → LLMs have less training data in non-English languages = different citation patterns
> → hreflang still matters for international signals
> → Local authority signals (local backlinks, local mentions) influence AI citations
> → Query patterns differ by language — don't just translate, localize intent
>
> For B2B SaaS going international:
> Don't translate your AI SEO strategy. Rebuild it for each market.
> The framework is the same. The execution is local.

**Key insight:** AI SEO is market-specific. Less LLM training data in non-English = different citation behavior. Localize intent, not just language.
