## Post 1 — Decoupling Clicks from Impact

**Date:** 2024  
**Theme:** How to measure SEO in the AI era

> Traffic is no longer a good proxy for SEO success.
> 
> Here's what I mean:
> 
> When AI Overviews answer questions directly, your traffic drops.
> But your brand might be getting mentioned in those answers.
> That's still impact — just invisible in GA4.
> 
> I call this the "decoupling of clicks from impact."
> 
> New metrics you need to track:
> → AI mention rate (how often does ChatGPT/Perplexity cite you)
> → Branded search volume (proxy for awareness even without clicks)
> → Conversion rate from the traffic that does click through
> 
> If you're only watching sessions, you're flying blind.

**Key insight:** AI search = fewer clicks but potentially higher brand impact. Need new measurement frameworks beyond GA4 traffic.

## Post 2 — What Working with Dropbox/Reddit Taught Me About AI SEO

**Date:** 2024  
**Theme:** Enterprise AI SEO lessons

> After years advising companies like Dropbox, Reddit, and Meta on organic growth, one pattern is clear:
> 
> Large companies lose AI search visibility because they publish for breadth, not depth.
> 
> Reddit is a perfect case study. Reddit itself is one of the biggest beneficiaries of AI Overviews — because Reddit content is deep, specific, and community-validated.
> 
> What that means for B2B SaaS:
> 
> → Depth beats volume every time in AI search
> → AI cites primary sources, not summaries
> → Topical specificity > topical breadth
> 
> The companies winning in AI search look more like Reddit threads than Wikipedia articles.

**Key insight:** AI systems prefer deep, specific, community-validated content over broad summaries. B2B SaaS content should mimic Reddit's specificity.

## Post 3 — The AI Search Content Checklist

**Date:** 2025  
**Theme:** Content optimization for AI engines

> Before publishing any piece of SEO content in 2025, run this check:
> 
> ✅ Is the primary source claim verifiable (not AI-synthesized)?
> ✅ Does the author have demonstrated expertise on this topic?
> ✅ Is the content cited on at least 2-3 external platforms?
> ✅ Does it answer the question directly in the first 100 words?
> ✅ Is there data or a case study no one else has?
> 
> If you can't check all 5 boxes, the content will struggle in both traditional search and AI answers.
> 
> E-E-A-T isn't a checklist Google made up. It's how AI systems evaluate trustworthiness.

**Key insight:** 5-point content checklist for AI era — verifiable sources, expert authorship, external citations, direct answers, unique data.
